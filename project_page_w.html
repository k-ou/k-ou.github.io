The Client

ACM-W @ UCLA (Association of Computing Machinery - Women) is an organization that supports those interested in computer science and endeavors to increase diversity in the technical field by providing a range of programs and services to UCLA students.

The Task

I was tasked with designing a shirt for ACM-W's members for the upcoming year.

Solution

One of ACM-W's primary goals is to cultivate a safe space for women who are interested in computer science, a subject, like most engineering fields, that is comprised mostly of men. They wanted to express a desire for a diverse community without seeming aggressive or depicting their mission or members as stereotypically feminine.

Instead of relying on gendered stereotypes to show diversity, I focused on how ACM-W wanted to build a more diverse community through sparking conversation — by networking, debating, learning, and teaching.
