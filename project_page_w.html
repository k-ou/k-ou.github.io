ACM-W @ UCLA T-shirt Design

The Client

ACM-W @ UCLA (Association of Computing Machinery - Women) is an organization that supports, celebrates, and advocates for the full engagement of all who are interested in computer science. Additionally, ACM-W endeavors to increase all aspects of diversity in the technical field by providing a range of programs and services to UCLA students.

The Task

I was tasked with designing a shirt for ACM-W's members for the upcoming year.

My Solution

One of ACM-W's primary goals was to cultivate a safe space for women who were interested in computer science, a subject, like most engineering fields, that is comprised mostly of men.

The challenge laid in expressing a desire for a diverse community without seeming aggressive or depicting our mission or members as stereotypically feminine. We wanted to be an organization that welcomed all people from all walks of life who were interested in furthering our ideal.

Initial sketches were either too superficial, drawing their roots from generic logos and lettering styles, or too polarizing, reminiscent of something you might see on tumblr.

Starting over, I considered what the goal of ACM-W ultimately was. Yes, we wanted a more diverse community in computer science. But the way we wanted to build that community would ultimately be through sparking conversation — by networking, debating, learning, and teaching.

My design was also used to promote their first general meeting and their general meetings since.
