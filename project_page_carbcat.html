CarbCat Goes to the Museum

The Client

Created for a history of graphic design class.

The Task

I was asked to compile examples of different pieces from each major art movement that I've learned about.

My Solution

Tasked with making a large set of images look like a cohesive group, I worked in the best way I knew how — I told a story.

I was immediately drawn to the idea of making a children's book. I didn't want the images to look catalogued; I wanted them to be expressive. I already had a main character in mind and sketched out my original character, CarbCat, finding a look for her that I felt was relatable and sympathetic.

I grouped the curated art pieces together based on a general feeling I got from them; perhaps they were uplifting or dark or shared some overarching theme. Having organized each of these themes into a conceivable plot, I was then faced with crafting the copy for the story itself, a challege that I didn't expect but one that I found the most rewarding in the end. Once I pinned down the storyline, illustrating the scenes was easy.

The final product was clean and whimsical and fully unique, and it displays both my active imagination and my skill as an illustrator.
