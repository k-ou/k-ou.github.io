Concept Cover

The Client

“Hillbilly Elegy: A Memoir of a Family and Culture in Crisis” by J.D. Vance is a memoir that recounts and considers the Appalachian values of the author's hometown.

The Task

I was tasked with designing a book cover using one typeface, simple shapes and a limited color scheme.

My Solution

The memoir tells a story of struggle and turmoil within an impoverished community, which has been lost to cynicism and despair. As such, I wanted show the tension and isolation through my composition.

I liked the contrast that a split complementary color scheme allowed, and I used jagged, irregular rectangles to create some semblance of the fibers of a screen door, fraying and weathered like the hopes of the community recounted in the book.
