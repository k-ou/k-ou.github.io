Imbue Film Festival

Imbue Seattle Filmmaker Series is a documentary film festival that focusues on visually stunning documentaries. Hosted at the Seattle Art Museum, Imbue celebrates truth in an artful way, recognizing filmmakers for their meaningful work and inspiring productive discourse among attendees.

By Any Other Name

We came up with an impressive list of abstract and/or cheesy nouns and verbs — luxe, focus, diffuse, rapt, ignite. We thought more about what it was that attendees would get from this particular festival experience. We would be showing films that were beautiful, sure... but more than that, we wanted people to be filled with feelings. We wanted them to be imbued with emotion.

Imbue was born.

Logo

Recognizing that the name we chose was itself already abstract and beautiful as it was, we opted for a wordmark to represent the festival. We played with different inks and brushes and different styles of writing until we found a mark we liked. It carried weight and motion, a scrawl like you might find at the bottom of a painting. A signature.

A Drop in the Bucket

Our visual research led us towards texture and motion, so we set off to find a visual style that would suit our festival. Suminagashi, the Japanese art of paper marbling, seemed to be a good fit. The process involves carefully dropping ink into water such that the ink remains on the surface; a paper is laid on the water and the ink is trasferred. If done well, a beautiful organic pattern is created.

From our experiments with suminagashi, we ended up with four transfers that we liked, which worked beautifully together or on their own. The rings felt like they were both receiving and emanating feelings, and they represented the idea of Imbue well. We digitally cleaned them up and added color, making each ink transfer distinct, but linked to another by a shared color of a single ring.

Connections

And after having found shapes that we liked, we wanted to find a way to connect them; just like when the feelings are shared through film, there is a connection happening between the director, the subjects in the film, and the audience. We recalled that while we were experimenting with suminagashi, we played with dragging brusshes through the water and ink mixture to create paths in the rings, and we were struck by the way the ink flowed along that path. We used brushes to create lines that would guide and interact with the four transfers we had made, and a harmony was formed.

#art

Since our target audience is the artsy urbanite, we figured that the best social media to target would be Instagram. Showcasing both our branding and the 10 films we're showing at the festival, the Instagram account is art all on it's own. The left column of the posts show a snippet from a prominent image from a film; looking at the individual post, you get more information on what it's about and can slide to view more of the image.
